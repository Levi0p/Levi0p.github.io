Research Thesis

Project Genesis

Toward Open-Ended Embodied Intelligence via Multi-Agent Continual Learning in Persistent Simulated Worlds — the long-term question underneath everything else on this site.

The Question

Can large language models evolve beyond static knowledge representations by coupling linguistic reasoning with persistent memory, environmental interaction, reinforcement learning, and decentralized multi-agent communication?

The Vision

Modern LLMs have extraordinary linguistic competence but remain static after training — they reason over text but don't continuously acquire knowledge through interaction with an environment. Human intelligence emerges from a lifelong cycle of perception, experimentation, memory consolidation, social communication, and adaptation.

Genesis is built around closing that gap: autonomous LLM agents inhabiting a persistent simulated world, acquiring increasingly sophisticated behavior through embodied interaction, reinforcement learning, long-term memory, and emergent social organization — asking whether intelligence can emerge from continuous experience, not just next-token prediction.

What's Actually In It

  • Cognitive architecture — episodic memory (specific experiences), semantic memory (generalized patterns), procedural memory (reusable skills), and a predictive world model, layered on top of a foundation LLM.
  • Persistent embodied environment — a continuous world, not isolated episodes: terrain, weather, scarce resources, and consequences that don't reset. Cut down a forest and it stays gone.
  • Hierarchical planning — immediate survival, intermediate goals like shelter and exploration, and long-term aims like settlement and technological advancement.
  • Continual reinforcement learning — agents keep optimizing policy through interaction instead of freezing after pretraining, while managing catastrophic forgetting.
  • Emergent multi-agent society — hundreds of agents with distinct memories and goals; the aim is to see whether specialization, trade, and cooperation emerge from the environment rather than being hand-coded.
Open-ended no final level, no win condition — success is continual emergence of more sophisticated behavior

Research Questions

  • Can persistent episodic memory improve long-term planning?
  • Does embodied interaction accelerate knowledge acquisition relative to passive language modeling?
  • Can decentralized communication produce collective intelligence exceeding individual agent capability?
  • Under what conditions do specialization and cooperation spontaneously emerge?
  • What architectural changes enable lifelong adaptation without catastrophic forgetting?

How This Actually Gets Built

The full architecture above is the destination, not the starting point — trying to build all of it at once is how ambitious research projects die. The plan is to earn it one convincing result at a time: start with a single LLM agent, one persistent world, basic memory and goal planning, and a handful of actions — enough to answer one sharp, measurable question, like whether episodic memory improves survival time. Every capability after that gets added only once the one before it is actually working.
